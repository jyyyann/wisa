2022 WISA Executive

With most of the WISA 2021 executive members heading to University, we need people to fill their roles so that WISA can keep running in 2022. Whether or not you are an international student, we welcome you to the family of our organization!


Listed below are the job vacancies and description, if any of them sounds like you, scroll down to the bottom of the page and nominate yourself via the Google form before 10th Feb!

We are looking for...

Secretary

   Wellington Central, Wellington           Administration

Are you someone who can communicate effectively? Are you a well organised person who can delegate tasks? If so, this role is perfect for you!


The duties of a Secretary include:

  • Preparing the agenda for executive meetings in consultation with the President
  • Making arrangements including venue, date, times and hospitality for club meetings
  • Minute-taking during executive meetings
  • Replying to emails and messages promptly on behalf of WISA


Treasurer

   Wellington Central, Wellington           Banking & Finance

Are you an Accounting student who is capable of handling figures? Can you make sure our events are under budget? If this sounds like you, nominate yourself to be a part of us this year!


The duties of a Treasurer include:

  • Managing funding, fundraising and sales
  • Financial planning and budgeting
  • Financial reporting during executive meetings
  • Maintain records by tracking every income and expense financial transaction


Event Coordinator

   Wellington Central, Wellington           Event Management

Are you a creative person who knows how to have fun? Have you had event management experience before? Nominate yourself now to be the "life and soul" of our committee.


The duties of an Event Coordinator include:

  • Advocating ideas for events
  • Planning event details and aspects, including venue, catering and guests
  • Writing run-downs and assigning jobs to other executive members
  • Ensuring everything runs smoothly during the events
  • Fulfilling the role of MC during events

Why be a part of WISA 2022?

1. Gain experience

Undoubtedly, holding leadership positions look good on a résumé. Whether you are applying for scholarship, internship, or a job in the future, having led a student organization shows that you’re hard working and can handle multiple responsibilities. Throughout the course of the year, you will learn a lot about event planning, financial budgeting, managing social media, contacting sponsorship, and more!

2. Build your network

WISA is the largest umbrella body that gather international students in the Wellington region. You will have the opportunity to connect with students from schools and countries other than your own. By working closely with each other, you'll build lifelong friendship with your fellow executive members. We have connection with Wellington NZ and Education NZ. We also collarboarte with the Interact Club of Port Nicholson every year.

3. Have fun!

While being a part of the executive team means a lot of dedication and committment, you will have a lot of fun working in a team. WISA gives you the platform to pitch and execute ideas you're passionate about, and back you up with manpower, resources, and budget. You will gain a great sense of accomplishment when you see the smiley faces on our event attendees. We also host exec-only gatherings from time to time in recognition of our hardwork.
