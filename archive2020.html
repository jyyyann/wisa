2020 Highlights

2020 was the birth year of WISA. We kicked off the year with a free movie night at the Penthouse Cinema, Brooklyn. The event was a huge success and attracted students from all over Wellington, who then become active members of our organisation. Subsequently, the second event- shared picnic & laser force was organised. In the same week, a Korean BBQ buffet was held. In August, we gathered again for an exciting bowling event. We made a come back in October for a sports day at Wellington College. We finished off the year collaborating with the Interact club for a quiz night fundraiser.

Quiz Night Fundraiser

We collaborated with the Interact Club of Port Nicholson Volunteering Club for a Quiz Night Fundraiser for Blue Children's Dragon Foundation, a charity in Vietnam that helps refugees and rescues children from slavery. Participants were tested on their knowledge of history, music, science, film, and more. Free pizza was provided for the night. Gift baskets were given out to the winners. The Ukelele club performed for the night.

Sports Day

We organised a sports day event at Wellington College for our members to have some fun. Free hot dogs were served. We set up mini-games, including tug of war, water bottle relay, and "eagle catches a chick". We set up the court for people to play ball sports. We invited Sarah Grant to our event and organised an information booth for international students. An "esports smash tournament" was also taken place.

Bowling Night

We hired lanes at The Lanes to host our bowling night event. We had a mix of international and domestic students participate in the event. Our participants were in a range ability levels in Bowling, but everyone had fun. Some stayed behind for billiard games.

Laser Force & Arcade Games

We walked our participants to Laserforce Wellington on Tory Street for Laser tag and Arcade games following the shared picnic. We negotiated for a discount for group booking and the price per person was reduced from $20.00 to $15.00. Laser tag is a recreational shooting sport where participants use infrared-emitting light guns to tag their opponents. There were also electronic basketball games, racing games, and other Arcade games.

Shared Picnic

We hosted a shared picnic at Waitangi Park, near the Waterfront. We prepared ice breaking events, card games, snacks, and ball sports for our participants. It was a fantastic opportunity to socialise with people from other schools and cultural backgrounds. It was also a beautiful day for some photoshoots.
